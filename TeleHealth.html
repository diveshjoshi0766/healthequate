TELEHEALTH


Telehealth, is defined as transmission of still images, patient’s consultations through video conferencing, patient portals, monitoring of vital signs, patient-focused wireless applications and many other applications.

Telehealth has many benefits for patients and physiotherapists.

The benefits to the patient include:-

• Removal of barriers such as travel time, parking, and waiting rooms
• More flexibility for patients as they can schedule therapy at times that suit them
• More cost-effective therapy in terms of travel, leaving work early or leaving children or family with a caregiver

The benefits to the physiotherapist physical therapist include:

• Improved efficiency as there is less travel between clinic locations

• Greater consistency in terms of patient self-management at home and monitoring standards of care and outcome

The consequences of COVID-19 will be specific in each individual and their rehabilitation needs will be specific to these consequences such as:

• Headaches
• Physical deconditioning
• Severe muscle weakness
• Reduced joint mobility
• Neck and shoulder pain (due to prone lying)
• Impaired balance and gait
• Impaired lung function
• Limitations of ADL
• Dysphagia
• Impaired swallow and communication

WHAT IS VIDEOPHYSIO?


The world of healthcare is constantly evolving. With the rise of technology, Physiotherapy is now available via video conferencing so that you can get treated for your injury and concerns from the comfort of home or if you’re away travelling. Over the last 5 to 10 years, Physiotherapy has moved away from a reliance on complex machines and manual techniques, and towards rehabilitation programs carried out under guidance. This puts you in much more control of your own recovery. Research has shown that outcomes can, in fact, be better through online consultation, due to the empowering nature of the system.

HOW DOES IT WORK?


Physiotherapy appointments start with a 'subjective assessment', in which the Physiotherapist will ask you a well-structured series of questions designed to discover what they need to know about your injury. By the end of the subjective assessment, the Physio should already have a very clear idea of your probable diagnosis. The online form you complete prior to your session will also guide the therapist to ask more detailed questions on your injury/issue and how it is impacting on your lifestyle. Following the subjective assessment, the Physio will observe you performing a range of tests and movements to confirm this diagnosis. The majority of musculoskeletal Injuries or issues are suitable for online Physiotherapy consultation. Evidence shows us that a detailed understanding of a patient’s injury is key when it comes to the diagnosis and management of most injuries.

IS VIDEOPHYSIO SUITABLE FOR ME?


VideoPhysio is suitable for everyone but is great for those that cannot make it into our clinic.

For example:

  • Those in isolation
  • Those who are immunocompromised
  • Those who have just had orthopaedic surgery
  • Those who are unable to travel due to time restrictions or location
  • Personal
  • The elderly
