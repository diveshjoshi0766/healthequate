MEDITATION


Meditation is nothing but a practice which, if done with an element of faith & discipline, has potential to self-conquer the body, mind & soul.

Studies have shown that regular meditation causes elevation in alpha waves in the brain. These alpha waves are related to higher functions like learning, creativity, increased focus and sense of accomplishment. The alpha waves are also related to peace, calmness and relaxation. Meditation sessions are useful to strengthen the inner self to focus better into work or personal life. These will be conducted at regular intervals to ensure there remains a sense of balance.


It helps an individual to understand and hence recognize his own emotions, that can be used to guide on future thinking and behaviour. This is known as Emotional Intelligence or Emotional Quotient.

Meditation helps us to discover ourselves, in addition to have an ability to read other’s emotions, and hence it helps us to stay away from negativity. Meditation literally wipes out mind related lifestyle problems like depression, anxiety, etc.

TWIN HEARTS


Meditating together with a group is much more powerful than meditation individually. According to Master Choa Kok Su, 7 people practicing the Twin Hearts Meditation together generates energy equal to 100 people practicing alone.

“When done regularly, Meditation on Twin Hearts will give you better physical, emotional, mental and spiritual health… When practised by many people, it can also miraculously heal the entire earth, thereby making the earth more harmonious and peaceful.” – Master Choa Kok Sui, Achieving Oneness with The Higher Soul​

Research and Scientific studies have been completed on the neurophysiological, psychological, sociological and psychospiritual effects of the Meditation on Twin Hearts. These studies have been conducted in the most part by Glenn Mendoza, M.D. in New York City, New York and by Dr. Vrunda and Supriya Ghorpadkar and Mr. Sundaram in Bangalore, India.

Meditation on Twin Hearts showed measurable changes on the pattern of electrical activity through the brain by electroencephalograph (EEG). Comparing EEF studies before and after meditation, there was a dramatic transition from beta waves to alpha waves and delta-theta waves in 14 meditators and non-meditators. There was an observable increase in brain synchrony (alpha-delta-theta waves) between the two hemispheres of the brain after the Meditation on Twin Hearts. There was synchronisation of brain waves initially between the same hemisphere and followed by synchrony between the frontal, middle and rear portions of the brain. There was a decrease in heart rate and respiratory rate of up to 15-20% and an increase in oxygen saturation after the Meditation on Twin Hearts.
